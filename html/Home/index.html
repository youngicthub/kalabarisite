About Kalabari Kingdom

The Kalabari Kingdom is a traditional state located in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria, specifically in Rivers State. Its history is rich and complex, intertwined with the broader history of the Niger Delta region. Origins: The Kalabari people are believed to have migrated from Ijaw communities in the Niger Delta region. They are part of the Ijaw ethnic group, which is one of the largest ethnic groups in the Niger Delta. The Kalabari settled along the banks of the New Calabar River, from which their name is derived. Formation of the Kingdom: The Kalabari Kingdom was established in the 17th century. Its formation was influenced by trade relationships with European traders, particularly the Portuguese and later the British. The Kalabari were active participants in the transatlantic slave trade, exchanging goods and captives with European traders. Social Structure: The Kalabari Kingdom was organized into a hierarchical social structure with a monarch, titled the Amayanabo, at the top. Below the monarch were titled chiefs, followed by various sub-chiefs and elders. The society was also organized into various guilds and societies, each with its own functions and responsibilities. Economic Activities: The Kalabari people were skilled traders and fishermen. They engaged in trade with neighboring communities and European traders, exchanging goods such as palm oil, ivory, and slaves.
